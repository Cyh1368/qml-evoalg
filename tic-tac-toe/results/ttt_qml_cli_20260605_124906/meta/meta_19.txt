# GLOBAL INSIGHTS SCRATCHPAD

The following are global insights about optimization approaches and their effectiveness:

## Successful Algorithmic Patterns  
- Parameterized CRX perimeter ring combined with a shared-parameter CRY hub entanglement.  
  • First realized in Generation 10 (Combined 0.60; train 0.57→val 0.65→test 0.50) and retained in the Current Best Program, this two-line swap from (static CZ + CRZ) to (CRX + CRY) yielded a +0.03 boost over prior bests.  
- Five-layer hierarchical rotation–entanglement–rotation block (pre-RZ → RY → entangle → post-RZ) repeated 3 uploads×2 repeats.  
  • Current Best (depth 70, 261 gates, 66 params) and all top contenders (Gen 10, Gen 12, Gen 13) share this backbone, balancing expressivity and trainability.  
- Symmetry-driven parameter sharing across corners/edges/center groups.  
  • Fixing N_PARAMS_PER_BLOCK=11 (total N_PARAMS=66) via shared RZ/RY rotations and single “crx_ring”/“cry_hub_edge” angles hit the performance sweet spot; deviations above/below 66 params degrade results.  
- Fixed re-upload (N_UPLOADS=3) and repeat (N_REPEATS=2) schedule.  
  • Retaining depth ≈ 70 consistently aligns with Combined ≥ 0.55; modifications to uploads or repeats correlate with both depth shifts and score drops.  

## Ineffective Approaches  
- Mixing multiple two-qubit parameter types or adding extra CZ layers.  
  • Gen 12 (ring CRX + hub CRX/CRY, 72 params) and Gen 15 (perimeter CZ + hub CRZ/CRX, 66 params) both capped at Combined 0.55; Gen 18’s added center–edge CZs plunged to Combined 0.46 with a large generalization gap (0.21).  
- Over-aggressive parameter sharing (under-parametrization).  
  • Gen 14 collapsed too many single-qubit angles (48 params) and fell to Combined 0.47 with gap 0.17, a clear underfitting signal.  
- Excessive parameters and depth (over-parametrization).  
  • Gen 13 doubled to 84 params and depth 132 but only reached Combined 0.56—diminishing returns beyond the 66-param, depth 70 regime.  
- Parameterized perimeter CRZ entanglement.  
  • Gen 6’s CRZ ring (66 params) underperformed the static-CZ ring (Combined 0.53 vs 0.57), reinforcing judicious placement of two-qubit parameters.  

## Implementation Insights  
- Centralized ANSATZ_SPEC builder enables rapid, low-risk gate-type swaps.  
  • In Gen 10/Current Best, switching perimeter CZ→CRX and hub CRZ→CRY was a two-line change in `_build_hierarchical_ansatz_spec()`, directly driving the +0.03 Combined improvement.  
- Deterministic parameter ordering via `dict.fromkeys` in `validate_ansatz_spec`.  
  • Guarantees N_PARAMS_PER_BLOCK=11 and consistent indexing across 3×2 blocks, simplifying `apply_ansatz_block` logic and avoiding off-by-one errors.  
- Early structural checks with `validate_ansatz_spec`.  
  • Catches non-adjacent grid edges or malformed gate entries before training, preventing wasted GPU/CPU time (no GRID_EDGE_SET violations in top programs).  
- Depth/gate profiling with `qml.queuing.AnnotatedQueue`.  
  • Ties circuit design directly to measured depth (70 gates) and gate_count (261), validating that the hierarchical 3×2 design matches the empirically optimal resource budget.  

## Performance Analysis  
- Peak Combined score (0.60) achieved at 66 params, depth 70.  
  • Current Best = Gen 10 outperforms the next best (Gen 17 & Gen 19 at 0.57) and worst (Gen 18 at 0.46).  
- Parameter sweet spot at 66:  
  • Reducing to 60 params (Gen 19) stalls at 0.57; increasing beyond (84 params in Gen 13) only hits 0.56.  
- Depth ≈ 70 remains optimal:  
  • Circuits deepened to 82–84 (Gen 18/19) underperform (0.46/0.57), while shallower or equivalent designs align with top scores.  
- Consistent parameter efficiency (~0.008 test_accuracy/param) across best programs.  
  • Maintained by the 3×2 hierarchical, symmetry-shared architecture, reflecting balanced utilization of every added parameter.

# META RECOMMENDATIONS

The following are actionable recommendations for the next program generations:

1. Insert a mid‐block RZ “phase‐alignment” layer immediately after the eight perimeter CRX gates by reusing the existing pre‐entanglement keys. In `_build_hierarchical_ansatz_spec`, append RZ rotations on corners (`rz_pre_corner`), edges (`rz_pre_edge`), and center (`rz_pre_center`) just before the hub CRY entanglement. This two‐line edit leverages proven symmetry‐driven sharing to condition the ring outputs without changing N_PARAMS=11 per block and tests whether extra phase alignment boosts validation accuracy.  

2. Swap the two‐qubit rotation axes between the ring and the hub entanglement to probe axis‐specific expressivity. In the ANSATZ_SPEC builder, change the ring gates from CRX→CRY (using key `cry_ring`) and the hub gates from CRY→CRX (using key `crx_hub_edge`), renaming the parameter keys accordingly but keeping all eight ring wires and four hub wires intact. This maintains depth 70 and 11 params per block while exploring whether reversing axis assignments yields a higher Combined score.  

3. Enhance non‐local mixing by appending two additional CRY diagonal entangling gates after each block’s hub CRY layer. In `_build_hierarchical_ansatz_spec`, after the four hub CRY entries, add CRY on wires `[1,3]` and `[5,7]` using the existing `cry_hub_edge` key. This symmetry‐preserving tweak introduces extra cross‐board correlations—shown beneficial in other top programs—without increasing the total parameter count or depth significantly.  

4. Reorder the entanglement sequence to test early central coupling. Modify the block spec so that the four hub CRY gates occur immediately after the RY rotations (Layer 2) and move the ring CRX gates (Layer 3) to follow. Because neither the number of gates nor the parameters change, this simple reordering evaluates whether prioritizing hub entanglement accelerates convergence or lifts validation performance.  

5. Introduce mild heterogeneity on the perimeter by splitting the ring parameter into two alternating angles. First, merge `rz_pre_center` and `rz_post_center` into a single key `rz_center` (freeing one param slot per block), then allocate that slot to a new `crx_ring_alt` parameter applied to every other CRX ring gate (e.g., gates on wires [1,2],[3,4],[5,6],[7,0]). This 1-for-1 swap keeps N_PARAMS_PER_BLOCK=11 (66 total) but tests whether alternating‐edge flexibility improves expressivity without over-parametrization.
