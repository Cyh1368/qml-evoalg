<DESCRIPTION>
Root cause: The ANSATZ_SPEC used independent RY parameters for each qubit, inflating the parameter count. While valid, this increases the search space and may hinder convergence. The validation system itself wasn’t pointed at this issue, but the seed could be improved by enabling parameter sharing to be more parameter-efficient and symmetry-friendly.

Fix: Modify the ANSATZ_SPEC within the EVOLVE-BLOCK so that all 9 RY gates share a single parameter name (ry_shared) within a block, while keeping rz_pre and rz_post gates and the CZ connections unchanged. This preserves the interface and training loop, but reduces the per-block parameter count from 27 to 19, improving efficiency and potentially reducing the training gap. All other gates remain the same and are unchanged to maintain the task setup.
</DESCRIPTION>
